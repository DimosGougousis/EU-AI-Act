1. General Description of the AI System

1.1 Intended Purpose

PulseCredit v2.1 is an AI system deployed by FinPulse NL B.V. for the automated assessment of individual creditworthiness and generation of credit recommendations for consumer loan applications. The system evaluates natural persons applying for consumer loans ranging from €500 to €25,000 via the FinPulse mobile application.

The system is used solely within the context of FinPulse's authorised Consumer Credit Provider activities under the Wet op het financieel toezicht (Wft) in the Netherlands.

2. Model Architecture & Training

2.1 Architecture

Model type: Two-stage ensemble — XGBoost gradient boosted decision tree (primary) with Logistic Regression calibration layer (secondary). Trained using supervised learning on historical loan application outcomes.

3.2 Known Limitations & Failure Modes

  • Thin-file applicants: Applicants with fewer than 6 months of BKR history and limited PSD2 transaction data receive less accurate scores. The model's Brier Score increases to 0.14 for this group (above threshold). These applicants are routed to the manual review path.
  • Self-employed income volatility: Self-employed applicants with irregular monthly income patterns may be underscored relative to their actual repayment capacity. DTI ratio may overestimate risk in months with lower declared income.
  • Recent life events: The model does not account for recent life events (job change, marriage, relocation) that occurred after the PSD2 data window. Loan officers are instructed to consider supplementary documentation in such cases.
  • Model drift trigger: PSI > 0.25 triggers automatic retraining review. Population shift detected and retraining initiated in Nov 2025 (v2.1.1).

4. Instructions for Deployers (Internal Use — FinPulse Loan Officers)

TopicGuidance
Intended usePulseCredit outputs are a decision support tool, not an autonomous decision-maker. Loan officers must apply professional judgment and are authorised to override the AI recommendation.
Interpreting the scoreScore 0–100 represents probability of default × 100. Score <20: low risk (Approve); 20–45: medium risk (Refer — human review required); >45: high risk (Decline). Thresholds are configurable by credit policy team.
SHAP explanationsThe top 5 feature contributions are displayed in the loan officer dashboard. Positive bars increase approval likelihood; negative bars increase decline likelihood. Officers must review SHAP output before confirming AI recommendation.
Override authorityLoan officers have full authority to override any AI recommendation. All overrides must be logged with a reason code. Override rates are reviewed monthly by the Head of Credit Operations.
Thin-file routingApplicants flagged as "thin-file" are automatically routed to manual review regardless of AI score. Officers may request additional documentation (bank statements, employment contract).
Prohibited actionsDo not use PulseCredit outputs for any purpose other than consumer credit decisioning. Do not share scores externally. Do not attempt to reverse-engineer individual feature weights.
