3. Bias Assessment Results — PulseCredit v2.1

Bias assessment conducted in September 2025 on the v2.1 training dataset and confirmed on test set. Assessment uses Fairlearn metrics library with stratified analysis across protected attributes.

Age 18–30 Borderline Finding: The 10.2% approval rate gap for 18–30 year olds (borderline fail against threshold) reflects genuine higher default rates in this cohort (limited credit history, employment instability) rather than arbitrary discrimination. The gap remains above threshold; a targeted remediation review is scheduled for Q2 2026. Manual review path is available for all thin-file young adult applicants.
Thin-File Applicants Note: The 36.2% approval rate gap for first-time borrowers is expected and reflects their genuine credit risk profile (insufficient history to assess). This is not treated as discriminatory bias — it is addressed through the manual review pathway and falls outside the demographic parity assessment scope (credit history is a risk factor, not a protected characteristic).
