1. Scope & Governance

This Risk Management System (RMS) is established for PulseCredit v2.1 in compliance with Article 9 of EU AI Act Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. It covers the full AI lifecycle from design through deployment through retirement, as required by Article 9(1): "A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems."

2. Risk Identification Methodology

Risks are identified across six threat categories using structured workshops (multi-disciplinary team: data science, engineering, compliance, legal, credit operations) supplemented by structured review of academic literature on credit AI risks and DNB/AFM supervisory guidance.

Bias & Discrimination

Risk of systematic unfair treatment of protected groups (gender, age, nationality, ethnicity by proxy).

Model Drift

Risk that model performance degrades as population characteristics change over time (economic cycles, product changes).

Data Quality

Risk of incorrect decisions due to corrupted, stale, or manipulated input data.

Cybersecurity

Risk of adversarial attacks, model poisoning, inference attacks, or unauthorised access to model outputs.

Fundamental Rights

Risk of violating rights (non-discrimination, privacy, access to financial services, right to explanation).

Operational

Risk of human oversight failure, override misuse, or system unavailability affecting lending decisions.
