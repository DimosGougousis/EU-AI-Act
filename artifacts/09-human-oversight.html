Human Oversight Design

PulseCredit v2.1 — Article 14, EU AI Act: Meaningful Human Control Over High-Risk AI

Artifact 09 | Version: 1.0-DRAFT | Oversight Owner: Head of Credit Operations

1. Oversight Model Selection

Article 14 requires that high-risk AI systems be designed to enable natural persons to effectively oversee the AI system's operation. FinPulse has selected a tiered Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) model with Human-in-Command (HIC) as the overarching governance principle.

Loans ≤ €5,000

HITL — Strategic
AI makes automated decision. Decision is logged and subject to retrospective human audit (1% monthly sample review by Head of Credit Operations). Applicant notified of AI use.

Loans > €5,000

HITL — Direct
AI generates recommendation + SHAP explanation. Loan officer reviews and must actively confirm, modify, or override before decision is issued. Override requires reason code.

Thin-File Applicants

Human-in-Command
Regardless of loan amount, thin-file applicants are always routed to a senior loan officer for full manual review. AI score displayed as advisory only.

System Unavailable

Human-in-Command
If PulseCredit is unavailable, all applications are queued for manual review. No automated decisions during system downtime. SLA: manual review within 1 business day.

Article 14(4) Requirement Met: The system is designed so that loan officers "are able to intervene" and "decide not to use" the AI output. The override mechanism ensures humans retain ultimate decision-making authority. FinPulse cannot reduce loan officers' authority to override by internal policy — this is a permanent system design requirement.

2. Loan Officer Dashboard Design

The loan officer decision interface is designed to present AI outputs in a way that enables meaningful oversight — not passive confirmation. Key design principles: (1) AI recommendation is never pre-selected; (2) SHAP explanation must be reviewed (tracked via UI interaction analytics); (3) Override button is equally prominent as confirm button.

3. Override Mechanism & Logging

ElementDesign
Override authorityAll loan officers have full authority to override. No approval needed from senior officer for override (removing friction that incentivises rubber-stamping).
Reason code requirementAny decision differing from AI recommendation requires mandatory reason code selection + optional free text. Codes: [Supplementary income documented], [Life event explained], [Additional security provided], [AI output appears erroneous], [Regulatory / policy exception], [Other — manual].
LoggingAll decisions logged: timestamp, application ID (pseudonymised), AI score, AI recommendation, loan officer decision, reason code, review duration. Retained 6+ months (aligned with Art. 12).
Override rate monitoringOverride rates reviewed monthly by Head of Credit Operations. Anomalies (e.g., individual officer override rate >60% without documented reason) trigger supervision review.
Audit trailImmutable audit log — loan officers cannot modify or delete decision records. Accessible to DNB/AFM on supervisory request.

Certification: 4-hour total (split over 2 half-days). Annual recertification (1-hour refresher). New hires complete training before accessing PulseCredit dashboard. Training records maintained in HR system (linked to AI compliance log).

Target completion: 100% of loan officers by June 30, 2026.
