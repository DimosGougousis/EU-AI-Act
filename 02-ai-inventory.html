Classification Methodology

Risk classification follows the four-tier framework established by Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the EU AI Act). Each AI system operated by FinPulse was assessed against:

  1. Article 5 — Prohibited AI practices (immediate disqualification if applicable)
  2. Annex III — Listed high-risk AI system categories (explicit enumeration)
  3. Recital 58 & Annex III footnotes — Specific exemptions (fraud detection carve-out)
  4. Article 50 — Limited-risk transparency obligations (chatbots, synthetic content)
  5. Residual category — Minimal-risk, no specific AI Act obligations
Key Principle — Profiling Escalation An AI system that otherwise falls outside Annex III may be re-classified as high-risk if it profiles individuals based on behaviour, economic situation, or personal characteristics to make or substantially influence decisions in an Annex III context. This principle was applied to PulseConnect.

Classification Rationale

PulseCredit — HIGH-RISK

EU AI Act Annex III, Point 5(b): "AI systems intended to be used for the purpose of evaluating the creditworthiness of natural persons or establishing their credit score, with the exception of AI systems used for the purpose of detecting financial fraud."

Analysis: PulseCredit is explicitly listed in Annex III. It evaluates the creditworthiness of natural persons (Dutch consumers) and generates a credit score that directly informs loan origination decisions. The system is used by FinPulse in its capacity as a Consumer Credit Provider (Wft licence).

Exemption check: PulseCredit is not a fraud detection system — it performs creditworthiness evaluation. The Recital 58 fraud exemption does not apply. The system falls squarely within Annex III §5(b).

Article 6 check: The system is used in the context of regulated financial services and makes or substantially influences decisions with legal or similarly significant effects on natural persons (credit approval/rejection). Article 6(2) confirms high-risk classification for Annex III listed systems used in such contexts.

Conclusion: HIGH-RISK — Full Article 16 provider obligations apply. Conformity assessment required by August 2, 2026.

PulseConnect — HIGH-RISK

EU AI Act Annex III, Point 5(b) + Article 6(2) + Recital 47: AI systems that profile individuals' economic situation, behaviour, or personal characteristics to inform decisions relating to credit access fall within the high-risk category, even when not directly issuing a credit decision themselves.

Analysis: PulseConnect's Financial Health Score is not, on its face, a credit decision — it is presented as a personalised financial insight. However, substantive analysis reveals that:

  • The score directly measures creditworthiness-relevant attributes (income stability, spending patterns, debt levels)
  • The "credit eligibility nudge" feature explicitly recommends specific credit products based on the score
  • Users who receive a positive nudge are statistically 4x more likely to apply for PulseCredit products
  • The system profiles individual economic behaviour continuously on 95,000+ users monthly

Boundary analysis — creditworthiness vs. financial wellness: The EU AI Act does not require a system to make a credit decision to be classified as high-risk under Annex III §5(b). A system that evaluates creditworthiness as part of its core function — even to produce a score or recommendation — falls within scope. PulseConnect's core output (Financial Health Score + credit eligibility) is functionally a creditworthiness evaluation.

Deployer note: FinPulse is the deployer — CreditIQ B.V. (the provider) bears primary compliance responsibility for the model. However, FinPulse must fulfil full Article 26 deployer obligations, including conducting a FRIA and ensuring end-users are notified of AI use in the eligibility assessment context.

Conclusion: HIGH-RISK — Article 26 deployer obligations apply. FinPulse must confirm CreditIQ B.V.'s provider compliance and implement deployer measures by August 2, 2026.

PulseGuard — EXEMPT from High-Risk

EU AI Act Recital 58: "AI systems used for the sole purpose of preventing, investigating, detecting or prosecuting criminal offences, or of executing criminal penalties, including safeguarding against and preventing threats to public security, should not be considered high-risk AI systems... Furthermore, AI systems used by financial institutions for the purposes of detecting financial fraud should not be considered high-risk AI systems."

Analysis: PulseGuard is operated exclusively for the purpose of detecting payment fraud and AML-suspicious transactions. Its outputs (risk scores and alerts) are used solely to flag potential fraud for human compliance review — not for any credit, eligibility, or commercial decisions. It fits squarely within the Recital 58 fraud detection exemption.

Important limitation: The exemption applies only when the system is used for the sole purpose of fraud detection. If PulseGuard's outputs were repurposed — e.g., to inform PulseCredit's credit scoring or to profile customers for marketing — the exemption would be lost and the system would need re-classification. This risk must be monitored through data use governance controls.

Applicable obligations:

  • Article 50(1) — Transparency: End-users should be notified that AI is used to monitor their transactions (already disclosed in FinPulse's Terms & Privacy Policy — confirmed compliant)
  • AML Directive (AMLD5/6), PSD2, and DNB AML supervisory requirements continue to apply in full

Conclusion: EXEMPT from high-risk classification. Article 50 transparency disclosure obligation applies and is currently met. No conformity assessment required.

PulseBot — LIMITED-RISK

EU AI Act Article 50(1): "Providers shall ensure that AI systems intended to interact directly with natural persons are designed and developed in such a way that the natural persons concerned are informed that they are interacting with an AI system, unless this is obvious from the circumstances and the context of use."

Analysis: PulseBot is a conversational AI system (chatbot) that interacts directly with natural persons via the FinPulse app. It provides informational customer support and does not make financial decisions or assess creditworthiness.

Annex III check: PulseBot does not fall within any Annex III category. It does not assess creditworthiness, determine eligibility for services, profile individuals for financial decisions, or perform any other listed high-risk function. It is an informational assistant only.

Article 50 obligation: As a chatbot interacting with natural persons, PulseBot must disclose its AI nature at session start. FinPulse currently implements this via an automatic disclosure message: "You are chatting with PulseBot, an AI assistant. Type 'agent' at any time to speak with a human."

Conclusion: LIMITED-RISK — Article 50(1) disclosure obligation only. Currently compliant. No conformity assessment required.
