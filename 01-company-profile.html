About FinPulse NL B.V.

FinPulse NL B.V. is a Dutch FinTech company incorporated in Amsterdam in 2021. Founded by a team of former banking technologists and data scientists, FinPulse provides a suite of AI-powered financial services to Dutch consumers and small businesses, operating under supervision of De Nederlandsche Bank (DNB) and the Autoriteit Financiële Markten (AFM).

The company holds a Payment Institution licence under PSD2 and a Consumer Credit Provider authorisation under the Wet op het financieel toezicht (Wft). As of 2026, FinPulse serves approximately 140,000 end-users and employs 85 full-time staff across product, technology, compliance, and operations.

FinPulse's competitive advantage rests on its use of machine learning across multiple financial service categories — including credit decisioning, personalised financial management, real-time fraud prevention, and customer support automation. This multi-product AI footprint makes EU AI Act compliance both critical and complex.

AI Product Portfolio

FinPulse operates four AI systems, ranging from Annex III high-risk credit decisioning to limited-risk customer chatbots. Each is described below with technical brief.

PulseCredit — AI Credit Scoring & Loan Origination

HIGH-RISK — Annex III §5(b) Provider (In-house)

Overview

PulseCredit is FinPulse's proprietary AI-driven credit underwriting engine. It automatically assesses individual creditworthiness and generates a credit score and loan eligibility decision for consumer loan applications submitted via the FinPulse mobile application. Loans range from €500 to €25,000.

Model Type

Gradient Boosted Decision Tree ensemble (XGBoost v1.7) stacked with a Logistic Regression calibration layer. Trained on 380,000 historical loan applications (2018–2024).

Inputs

  • Application data (age, employment status, income)
  • Credit bureau data (BKR score, payment history)
  • Open banking transaction history (12-month PSD2 feed)
  • Requested loan amount & term
  • Debt-to-income ratio

Output & Decision Role

Produces a probability-of-default score (0–100) and a recommended decision: Approve / Refer / Decline. For loans ≤ €5,000: automated decision with human audit trail. For loans > €5,000: human loan officer review required.

Affected Users

~18,000 loan applicants per year, all Dutch residents aged 18+.

PulseConnect — Open Banking PFM & Financial Health AI

HIGH-RISK — Annex III §5(b) Deployer (Third-party model)

Overview

PulseConnect aggregates user bank accounts via PSD2 open banking APIs and uses a third-party AI model (supplied by CreditIQ B.V., a Dutch AI vendor) to generate a personalised Financial Health Score (0–850) and credit eligibility nudges — e.g., "You are likely eligible for a €3,000 loan at 6.9% APR." These nudges influence whether users apply for PulseCredit products.

Model Type

Third-party SaaS API (CreditIQ HealthScore Engine v3.2) — neural network architecture per provider documentation. FinPulse has deployer obligations only.

Inputs

  • Bank transaction history (PSD2 feeds, up to 24 months)
  • Account balances across linked accounts
  • Spending category classifications
  • Income and salary pattern detection

Output & Decision Role

Financial Health Score + credit eligibility recommendation displayed in the app. Not legally binding but directly influences user behaviour and loan application rates. Classified high-risk because it functionally assesses creditworthiness (Annex III §5(b)).

Affected Users

~95,000 active PulseConnect users monthly.

PulseGuard — Payment Fraud Detection & AML Monitoring

EXEMPT — Recital 58 Fraud Detection Deployer (SaaS)

Overview

PulseGuard uses a third-party SaaS fraud and AML detection platform (FraudSense EU, operated by Threatrix B.V.) to monitor real-time payments processed by FinPulse and identify suspicious transaction patterns. Alerts are reviewed by FinPulse's compliance team before any account action is taken.

Model Type

Real-time anomaly detection combining rule-based engines with a graph neural network (GNN) for transaction network analysis. Third-party SaaS — FinPulse is deployer only.

Inputs

  • Transaction amount, currency, timestamp
  • Sender/receiver account identifiers (pseudonymised)
  • Merchant category codes
  • Device fingerprinting data (mobile app)
  • Historical transaction velocity patterns

Output & Decision Role

Risk score per transaction (0–100) + alert flag (Low / Medium / High). Alerts queued for compliance officer review — no automated blocking. Final action decided by humans only.

Regulatory Note

Explicitly exempt from high-risk classification under EU AI Act Recital 58. Only Art. 50 transparency disclosure applies.

PulseBot — Customer Service AI Chatbot

LIMITED-RISK — Art. 50(1) Provider (In-house)

Overview

PulseBot is a conversational AI chatbot embedded in the FinPulse mobile app and web portal. It handles customer queries about account status, product information, payment troubleshooting, and loan application status. Complex queries are escalated to human agents.

Model Type

Fine-tuned large language model (LLM) based on a commercial foundation model, with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) over FinPulse's FAQ and policy knowledge base.

Inputs

  • User text messages (natural language)
  • Account context (with user consent)
  • Knowledge base retrieval

Output & Decision Role

Natural language responses. No financial decisions — information and guidance only. Escalates to human agent on request or for complex matters.

Compliance Status

Art. 50(1) disclosure implemented: every PulseBot session opens with "You are chatting with an AI assistant. Type 'agent' at any time to speak with a person."

EU AI Act Actor Classification

The EU AI Act distinguishes between providers (who develop and place AI systems on the market) and deployers (who use AI systems in their operations). FinPulse occupies both roles across its product portfolio.

SystemActor RoleAI Owner / SupplierPrimary ObligationsKey Liability
PulseCredit Provider FinPulse NL B.V. (in-house developed) Art. 16: Risk management, data governance, technical documentation, logging, human oversight, conformity assessment Full provider liability for system defects and non-compliance
PulseConnect Deployer CreditIQ B.V. (third-party SaaS) Art. 26: Use per instructions, data quality, logging, human oversight, end-user notification, FRIA Liable for misuse and deployer obligation breaches; provider (CreditIQ) liable for system defects
PulseGuard Deployer Threatrix B.V. (FraudSense EU SaaS) Art. 50: Disclosure only (not high-risk). Existing AML/PSD2 obligations apply independently. Liable for AML regulation compliance; AI Act exposure minimal
PulseBot Provider FinPulse NL B.V. (in-house, LLM fine-tuned) Art. 50(1): Chatbot disclosure to natural persons interacting with the system Must clearly disclose AI nature at session start; currently compliant
Important — Deployer Becoming Provider If FinPulse significantly modifies or repurposes the CreditIQ model used in PulseConnect — for example by retraining it on FinPulse data — FinPulse would become the provider and assume full Article 16 obligations. This risk must be monitored as product teams customise integrations.

Dutch Regulatory Landscape

DNB — De Nederlandsche Bank

Banking supervisor. Oversees PulseCredit (consumer credit), AML/CFT compliance, and DORA ICT risk. Published SAFEST AI principles (2019). Joint AI oversight with AFM per 2024 AI report.

AFM — Autoriteit Financiële Markten

Markets regulator. Oversees FinPulse's payment institution licence and consumer credit conduct. Will supervise EU AI Act compliance for payment and credit services alongside DNB.

AP — Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens

Dutch Data Protection Authority. Supervises GDPR compliance for all personal data processing. Will support AI Act supervision in collaboration with RDI per EU AI Act coordination framework.

RDI — Rijksinspectie Digitale Infrastructuur

Dutch Market Surveillance Authority under the EU AI Act. Designated national AI authority. Coordinates enforcement with DNB and AFM for financial services AI systems.
