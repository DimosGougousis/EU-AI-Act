Implementation Roadmap

Phased compliance plan: February 2026 → August 2, 2026 deadline.

EU AI Act Assessment | Section 04 | Target: Full compliance by August 2, 2026

Home › Implementation Roadmap
⏱ Time Remaining: 157 days (as of February 26, 2026) High-risk AI obligations under Article 16 (provider) and Article 26 (deployer) become enforceable on August 2, 2026. All conformity assessments, documentation, and operational changes must be complete before this date.

Phased Implementation Timeline

Phase A
Foundation & Governance
February – March 2026 (Weeks 1–4)
  • Appoint dedicated AI Governance Officer (reporting to CRO)
  • Establish AI Governance Committee (CRO, CTO, DPO, Head of Compliance, Head of Legal)
  • Formally sign off AI inventory and risk classifications (Section 02)
  • Issue formal gap analysis report to Board (Section 03)
  • Initiate legal review of CreditIQ B.V. contract — request AI Act compliance warranties
  • Contact CreditIQ: request Annex IV technical documentation and conformity evidence
  • Begin GDPR DPIA update for PulseCredit (trigger: automated credit decisions)
  • Add PulseGuard in-app AI disclosure (quick win — 1-2 day development effort)
  • Archive PulseBot disclosure as baseline compliant — document evidence
Phase B
Documentation & Data Audit
March – May 2026 (Weeks 5–12)
  • Conduct PulseCredit training data audit: source identification, lineage documentation
  • Commission bias assessment: demographic parity and equalized odds across age, gender, nationality
  • Apply bias mitigations where assessment identifies issues (reweighting, feature removal)
  • Draft Annex IV technical documentation for PulseCredit (Artifact 06)
  • Establish formal risk management system for PulseCredit (Artifact 07)
  • Create data governance documentation (Artifact 08)
  • Design HITL human oversight workflow (Artifact 09) — stakeholder review with loan officers
  • Initiate FRIA for PulseCredit (Artifact 10)
  • Conduct FRIA for PulseConnect (separate effort — deployer perspective)
  • Enhance PulseCredit logging infrastructure: extend retention to 12 months, add model version tracking
  • Enhance PulseConnect logging: extend to 6 months, add Financial Health Score capture
Phase C
Testing, Review & Assessment
May – July 2026 (Weeks 13–22)
  • Commission adversarial robustness testing for PulseCredit (external vendor)
  • Conduct ML pipeline security assessment (DORA/AI Act Art. 15 overlap)
  • Document testing results in technical file
  • Finalise FRIA for PulseCredit — sign-off by DPO and CRO
  • Finalise FRIA for PulseConnect — sign-off by DPO
  • Complete human oversight workflow development and deploy to UAT environment
  • Train 100% of loan officers on AI literacy programme (4-hour certification)
  • Conduct loan officer UAT for HITL oversight workflow
  • Conduct internal conformity assessment (Annex VI) for PulseCredit
  • Legal review of Instructions for Use document
  • Draft EU Declaration of Conformity for PulseCredit
  • Review CreditIQ compliance package (if received) and document deployer due diligence
  • Update rejection/approval letters with AI disclosure and explanation mechanism
  • Legal review of all end-user-facing AI disclosures
Phase D
Go-Live Compliance
July 1 – August 2, 2026 (Weeks 23–26)
  • Final board sign-off on PulseCredit conformity assessment and Declaration of Conformity
  • Activate HITL oversight workflow in production (replace interim process)
  • Activate updated loan decision letters with AI disclosure
  • Activate PulseConnect "Generated by AI" label for Financial Health Score
  • Notify DNB and AFM of compliance status per regulatory engagement plan
  • Publish internal AI Register accessible to supervisory authorities
  • Confirm all technical documentation archived and accessible for audit
  • Final cross-check: all 21 gap items from Section 03 remediated
Phase E
Ongoing Obligations (Post-August 2026)
August 2026 onwards
  • Quarterly model performance reviews (AUC, GINI, PSI) — alert if thresholds breached
  • Monthly bias monitoring reports (demographic parity checks on live decisions)
  • Log retention management (minimum 6-month rolling window maintained)
  • Incident reporting: notify DNB/AFM within 72 hours of serious AI malfunction
  • Annual AI compliance review (full re-assessment against AI Act obligations)
  • Reassess classification if significant model changes or new use cases added
  • Track EU AI Act implementation guidance updates (AI Office)
  • Monitor CreditIQ compliance updates and provider obligation fulfillment
