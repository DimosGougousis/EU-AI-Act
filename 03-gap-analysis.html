PulseConnect — Deployer Gap Analysis HIGH-RISK

As deployer of CreditIQ's model, FinPulse must fulfil Article 26 obligations. Critically, FinPulse must also obtain evidence that CreditIQ (the provider) has fulfilled its Article 16 obligations.

EU AI Act Obligation Article Current State Gap Severity Remediation Action Owner Target Date
Obtain Provider Compliance Evidence Art. 26(1) CreditIQ contract does not include AI Act compliance warranties. No technical documentation received from CreditIQ. FinPulse has no evidence that CreditIQ has fulfilled Art. 16 obligations. Cannot fulfil deployer obligations without this. CRITICAL Formally request CreditIQ's Annex IV technical documentation and conformity assessment. Update contract with AI Act compliance warranties. If CreditIQ cannot demonstrate compliance, consider alternative provider or in-house replacement. Head of Legal / Vendor Management 31 Mar 2026
Use Within Instructions Art. 26(1) PulseConnect uses CreditIQ's API per commercial integration guide. No formal "Instructions for Use" document received from CreditIQ. Deployment may not align with CreditIQ's intended use boundaries. Cannot verify compliance without Instructions for Use. HIGH Obtain Instructions for Use from CreditIQ. Review current deployment against stated intended use. Head of Product (Connect) 31 Mar 2026
Data Quality Control Art. 26(3) PSD2 data fed to CreditIQ API is not validated against CreditIQ's data quality requirements (not provided by CreditIQ). Input data quality may not meet provider's requirements, leading to degraded or unreliable outputs. MEDIUM Implement data validation pipeline for PSD2 inputs. Align with CreditIQ data specification once obtained. Head of Data Engineering 30 Apr 2026
Logging of Operations Art. 26(5) PulseConnect logs API calls to CreditIQ but only retains logs for 14 days (data cost constraints). Log retention below 6-month minimum. Logs insufficient for audit. HIGH Extend retention to 6+ months. Log: user ID (pseudonymised), Financial Health Score output, nudge displayed, timestamp, CreditIQ API version. Head of Engineering 30 Apr 2026
Human Oversight Implementation Art. 26(2) Financial Health Score and eligibility nudges are fully automated. No human review of individual outputs. No human oversight mechanism in place. Mass deployment to 95,000 users without oversight review. For nudges above a credit threshold, human spot-check required. MEDIUM Implement sample-based human review of Financial Health Scores (e.g., 1% monthly sample). Define escalation for edge cases (scores near boundaries). Head of Product (Connect) 30 Jun 2026
Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment Art. 27 No FRIA conducted for PulseConnect despite credit eligibility outputs affecting 95,000 users. Missing FRIA — particularly important given potential for financial exclusion and discrimination in credit eligibility nudges. MEDIUM Conduct FRIA for PulseConnect, focusing on credit access and non-discrimination impacts on mass user base. DPO / Legal 31 May 2026
End-User Notification Art. 26(7) PulseConnect app shows Financial Health Score without disclosing AI generation. Users unaware score is generated by third-party AI system. No disclosure that AI (CreditIQ model) generates the score. Users not informed of AI's role in credit eligibility nudges. MEDIUM Add "Generated by AI" label to Financial Health Score display. Add tooltip/link to AI explanation page. Update Terms & Privacy Policy. Head of Product (Connect) / Legal 31 Jul 2026

Regulatory Overlay — DORA & GDPR Intersections

EU AI Act RequirementIntersecting RegulationOverlap / Integration Note
Art. 15 — Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity DORA (2022/2554) — ICT Risk Management (Arts. 5–16) AI system security and resilience testing should be integrated into FinPulse's DORA ICT risk framework. Avoid duplicate documentation — a single ICT risk assessment can satisfy both DORA ICT requirements and AI Act Art. 15 for cybersecurity. DORA incident reporting may overlap with AI Act serious incident reporting.
Art. 27 — Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment GDPR Art. 35 — Data Protection Impact Assessment The FRIA and DPIA are distinct but closely related. The GDPR DPIA for PulseCredit should be updated to cross-reference FRIA findings. Art. 35 DPIA obligations are triggered by automated credit decisions. The FRIA must cover fundamental rights beyond data protection. Both documents should be jointly maintained by the DPO and Legal team.
Art. 12 — Logging and record-keeping GDPR Art. 5(1)(e) — Storage limitation; Consumer Credit Directive record-keeping AI decision logs must be retained for minimum 6 months per AI Act. Consumer credit regulations may require longer retention (typically 5–7 years for credit decisions). FinPulse should align log retention policy to longest applicable period. Ensure logs are pseudonymised to balance GDPR data minimisation with AI Act audit requirements.
Art. 26(1) — Use per provider instructions GDPR Art. 28 — Data Processor Agreements CreditIQ B.V. (PulseConnect provider) must be a GDPR data processor for the personal data it processes. The existing data processing agreement should be reviewed to ensure it covers AI Act obligations alongside GDPR DPA requirements. CreditIQ must be contractually required to provide conformity documentation.
